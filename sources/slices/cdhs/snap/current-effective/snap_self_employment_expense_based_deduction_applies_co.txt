Colorado SNAP self-employment expense deduction rule under 10 CCR 2506-1
section 4.403.11(B)-(C)(3) for period 2026-01.

Use monthly SNAP period 2026-01.

Colorado Secretary of State, Code of Colorado Regulations 10 CCR 2506-1,
section 4.403.11:

Allowable costs of doing business include, but are not limited to: identifiable
costs of labor; stock; raw materials; seed; fertilizer; payments on the
principal of the purchase price of income producing real estate; capital
assets, equipment, machinery and other durable goods; interest paid to
purchase income-producing property; insurance premiums; taxes paid on
income-producing property; and, other similar items that are necessary costs
of doing business.

The following shall not be allowed as a deduction for business costs:

Federal, state, and local income taxes, money set aside for retirement
purposes, and other work-related personal expenses (such as meals). These
expenses are accounted for by the earned income deduction. However, any taxes
paid by the business for employees (such as the business share of social
security taxes) are allowed as a business deduction.

Model `snap_self_employment_expense_based_deduction_applies` as whether
Colorado allows actual self-employment business expenses to be deducted under
current-effective SNAP policy.
